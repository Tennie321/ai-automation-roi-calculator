Frequently Asked Questions

What exactly does the ROI Calculator measure? ▼
The calculator measures the total financial return on investment for implementing AI automation. It tracks three primary value streams: direct labor cost savings (hours reclaimed × hourly rate), productivity gain value (faster task completion enabling more output), and error reduction savings (fewer mistakes, higher quality). It then compares these benefits against the implementation cost and ongoing subscription to produce Year 1 ROI, ongoing annual ROI, break-even timeline, and multi-year projections.
Do I need to know Python to use this? ▼
Basic terminal familiarity is helpful, but the tool includes an interactive mode that prompts you for each input. No coding required. The online calculator on this page also works entirely in your browser with no installation needed. For the full CLI tool, you just need Python 3.8+ installed — no external packages required.
Can I use this in client proposals? ▼
Absolutely — this is one of the primary use cases. Agency owners and consultants use the ROI reports as social proof in their proposals. The clean formatted output, JSON/CSV exports, and investment grading make it easy to include in pitch decks and proposal documents. The tool is licensed for commercial use.
How accurate are the projections? ▼
The calculator uses conservative default assumptions (50% of theoretical productivity gain value, 5% error cost baseline) to avoid over-promising. The accuracy depends on the quality of your inputs. For best results, use actual salary data (not estimates), track current time spent on processes, and get real quotes for implementation costs. The tool is designed as a decision-support tool, not a financial guarantee.
What if the ROI is negative? ▼
The tool is honest about negative or marginal returns. It includes actionable recommendations for improving ROI, such as targeting higher-hour processes first, reducing implementation scope, or negotiating better subscription pricing. Sometimes the right answer is "don't automate this yet" — and the calculator helps you identify that before you invest.
